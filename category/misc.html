A Culinary Journey at Pausa in Tavira

Nestled along a quiet, almost idyllic road that felt more like a quaint square, Pausa in Tavira is an unassuming and unpretentious tapas restaurant that will surprise you with its genuine charm. Bathed in the scorching embrace of the sun (seriously, look out for the singed hairs on your head), and sheltered by the makeshift esplanade, it provided a welcome respite on a blissfully sunny day.

Setting the Scene

Though the interior of the restaurant lacks a certain sparkle, here's a small secret - you don't need it. The real magic happens outside in the tête-à-tête with your favorite Spanish flavors. We cozied up by the roadside, beneath the shade of the ivy, embracing the peaceful ambiance and absorbing the gentle movements of the few passing vehicles.

The Warm Welcome

The other patrons, mostly expats seeking culinary delights and shade, dotted the tables with their children in tow. The friendly staff effortlessly moved between tables, engaging with the little ones, and adding a touch of warmth (both figurative and literal) to the already inviting atmosphere. They entertained with their bright smiles, though fluency in Portuguese and English remained elusive for them - an endearing quirk that gifted us a genuine experience.

An Unexpected Culinary Adventure

As our appetite grew restless, curiosity ignited within us, beckoning us to embark on a delightful culinary adventure at Pausa in Tavira. In a charming twist of contemporary convenience, both the QR code menu and the classic printed menu on the wall presented us with a quirky challenge - they were outdated. However, fret not, for a new plastic menu was expediently provided, complete with updated prices that elicited a cheeky chuckle. Amidst the tiny uprising of our thrifty souls, we found solace in the fact that even with the adjusted figures, Pausa remained an affordable haven for our taste buds. With a sense of light-heartedness, we delved headfirst into a tantalizing world of Spanish tapas with a Colombian twist, eager to discover the flavors that awaited us.

The Food Journey

Let's start with the Spanish-style ham croquettes, a decadent rendition of this classic tapas dish. The golden exteriors were temptingly crisp, exuding a delicate smokiness. As we bit into the croquettes, we were greeted with a burst of saltiness from the ham, perfectly balanced by the creamy interior. Each bite brought us closer to culinary bliss.

Next up was the chevre wrapped in a pastry dough, almost like an empanada. The delicately flaky crust enclosed the tangy cheese and the luscious sweetness of the pumpkin marmalade. It was a texture party in our mouths, with the creamy chevre contrasting with the tender pastry. While it didn't steal the show, the dish held its own in the procession of flavors.

The batatas bravas, a tapas staple, unfortunately fell a bit short of our expectations. The bravas sauce lacked the vibrant flair we had anticipated. Despite this setback, there was a glimmer of hope as the sauce still teased our taste buds with its subtle spiciness. The potatoes themselves were tender and satisfying, albeit lacking the boldness we had hoped for.

Ah, the highlight - the codfish pataniscas. This dish captured the essence of the sea, transporting us to the sun-kissed shores of coastal Portugal. The fresh cod, enveloped in a delicate batter, was flecked with the brightness of fresh herbs. Their presence added an extra layer of freshness, perfectly balancing the sweetness of the onion. Each mouthful was a delicious reminder of seaside delights, a symphony of flavors that ignited our spirits.

Sweet Endings

With our savory cravings satiated, we eagerly delved into the realm of dessert. The pistachio tiramisu was a revelation, a symphony of flavors and textures that made our hearts sing. The sweetness was perfectly balanced, never overwhelming the palate. The pistachios added a delightful crunch, melding with the decadent creaminess of the tiramisu. It was a dessert to remember, leaving us with a lingering sweetness.

Lastly, we sampled the lime mousse, a refreshing palate cleanser. While undeniably silky and indulgent, it lacked the desired punchiness we had anticipated. The lime essence was gentle rather than vibrant, its tang subdued. We longed for a more pronounced citrus zing, a burst of lime that would awaken our taste buds.

Conclusion

With unabashed devotion to authenticity, Pausa - a fitting name meaning "to pause" or "break" in Portuguese - offers an ideal destination for a laid-back lunch. Enticed by their Colombian chef's deft touch, as well as the stunning simplicity of presentation that numbs no ambitions, you'll find yourself surrendering gladly to the captivating realm of Spanish tapas with a twist. Let Pausa, nestled beneath the embrace of ivy, be your serene haven in Tavira, where you can take a leisurely pause, unwind, and delight in the unpretentious pleasures of food done right.
